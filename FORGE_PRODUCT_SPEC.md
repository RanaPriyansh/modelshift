**Status:** Design baseline; implementation and efficacy unproven  
**Version:** 0.2

**Date:** 23 July 2026
**Scope:** The humane learning architecture named FORGE  
**Normative words:** **MUST**, **MUST NOT**, **SHOULD**, and **MAY** have their usual requirements meaning.

## 0. Authority, history, and claim boundary

This document governs the FORGE product direction. Repository-level operating authority and document precedence remain defined by the current `AGENTS.md`. `FINAL_PRODUCT_SPEC.md` is an unchanged historical ModelShift v1 artifact: evidence of a useful mechanics prototype contract, not authority for or proof of the broader FORGE system.

The four governing FORGE documents are:

1. `FORGE_PRODUCT_SPEC.md` — product purpose, rights, promises, boundaries, and acceptance contract.
2. `docs/FORGE_ARCHITECTURE.md` — service, data, state, safety, and observability architecture.
3. `docs/FORGE_RESEARCH_TO_SYSTEM.md` — traceability from the local research reports and current research amendments to system countermeasures.
4. `docs/FORGE_DELIVERY_GATES.md` — delivery phases, evaluations, stop rules, and permitted claims.

The strategy and programme documents `docs/program/AI_ERA_LEARNING_THESIS.md`, `docs/program/FOUNDER_IDEA_LOG.md`, and `docs/program/WAVE_6_PLAN.md` preserve the founder direction, current research synthesis, and proposed execution sequence. They do not supersede the four governing documents or authorize implementation, providers, publication, research, or release by themselves.

When they conflict, this product specification governs product intent and rights; the architecture governs technical interpretation; the delivery gates govern whether a claim may be made. A later change to a child right, evidence standard, or constitutional invariant requires an explicit decision record and review by product, learning, safety/privacy, accessibility, and domain owners.

**Exact claim boundary today:** FORGE is a proposed broad architecture informed by local research syntheses, current primary sources, and a bounded working engineering foundation. No broad educational efficacy, child-safety operation, privacy compliance, source accuracy, assessment validity, human network, homeschool quality, universal topic coverage, or production-scale claim has yet been demonstrated. The existing bounded implementation and historical ModelShift World may be cited only for their exact accepted engineering behaviors, never as validation of the broader FORGE thesis.

## 1. Product in one sentence

**FORGE is a learner-owned operating system for building foundations, pursuing deep questions, completing consequential projects with people, and producing independent evidence of capability—while governing AI so that it expands access without taking over the thinking a person is trying to learn.**

## 2. The problem FORGE exists to solve

Education currently bundles capability formation, care, civic membership, coordination, and signalling. Those public and human functions remain necessary. Yet many learners experience a recurring gap:

- time advances while prerequisite knowledge remains insecure;
- explanations and answers are abundant, but durable understanding is not;
- projects arrive without foundations, or foundations never become meaningful work;
- personalization becomes a private playlist rather than access to a shared entitlement;
- polished assisted output is mistaken for learner-owned capability;
- tests, grades, completion, and engagement become proxies for learning;
- knowledge is detached from physical reality, people, responsibility, and consequential use;
- family exit from school can improve fit while creating new risks in breadth, relationships, safeguarding, and portability;
- adults must continually relearn, but most systems are organized around one early-life credential sequence.

FORGE supplies the missing connective architecture. It does not claim to replace every school function, family, teacher, peer group, public institution, workplace, or community. It replaces the weak progression logic that equates exposure and completion with growth, and it coordinates external human and institutional resources when software is not the right provider.

## 3. Product promise

FORGE promises a learner:

> You will know what you are trying to become able to do, receive enough structure and help to make progress, retain ownership of the important thinking, meet real standards and real people where appropriate, and leave with inspectable evidence of what you can do—with and without tools.

FORGE does **not** promise that every learner will master every topic, that AI can replace a qualified adult, that one sequence works in every domain, or that a portfolio entry is equivalent to a regulated credential.

## 4. Who FORGE serves

FORGE supports a lifelong range, with different authority and safety regimes rather than one interface stretched across ages.

| Band | Default relationship to FORGE | AI boundary | Human authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early childhood, approximately 3–6 | Adult-facing planning and shared, brief activities; no independent AI relationship | No unsupervised conversational AI, profiling, or personal memory | Guardian/educator initiates and remains present; physical play, language, care, and relationships dominate |
| Foundation years, approximately 7–9 | Closed, authored learning worlds and offline practice | AI normally behind the interface; no open chat or open web; attempt before help | Verified guardian account and age-appropriate learner assent for persistence |
| Upper primary, approximately 10–12 | Guided foundations, labs, small inquiries, and supervised projects | Curated sources, bounded transformations, visible citations, short memory | Guardian controls external sharing, people contact, and sensitive data; learner can inspect and contest evidence |
| Middle secondary, approximately 13–15 | Increasingly independent labs, inquiry, projects, and AI literacy | Guardrailed research and creation; protected closed-tool practice; no hidden answer access | Guardian consent where required; learner voice and privacy increase; adults receive summaries, not surveillance |
| Upper secondary, approximately 16–17 | Complex projects, external critique, pathway exploration, professional AI modes | Wider tools with disclosure, source verification, risk gating, and independent defence | Minor safeguards remain; guardian powers cannot erase the learner's rights or secretly monitor all work |
| Adult, 18+ | Self-governed learning, reskilling, research, projects, and portable evidence | User selects declared assistance mode within safety and integrity constraints | Learner is the primary authority; institutions receive only explicit, purpose-bound shares |

Age is not a proxy for readiness or worth. Accessibility, language, prior knowledge, and local law change delivery, but never justify lowering the learner's right to clarity, correction, privacy, or a broad foundation.

## 5. Product constitution

Every FORGE experience MUST satisfy these constitutional rules.

1. **A broad entitlement comes before hyper-personalization.** Literacy, numeracy, disciplinary knowledge, civic understanding, health, computing/AI literacy, arts, physical and practical competence remain visible. Personalization changes time, support, representation, and route—not the learner's future options in secret.
2. **The learner acts before AI performs the target operation.** The system obtains a prediction, attempt, plan, retrieval, source judgment, design choice, or explicit “I do not know” before cognitive assistance, unless safety or access requires otherwise.
3. **Knowledge and agency are complements.** FORGE explains, models, and guides novices; it releases responsibility as evidence grows. It rejects both endless instruction and unguided discovery.
4. **The world can disagree.** Consequences come from validated code, inspectable data, credible sources, physical observation, external standards, or accountable human review—not model confidence or praise.
5. **Help is visible and governed.** Assistance mode and meaningful support are recorded. Accessibility support is not mislabeled as cognitive dependency. A request for a full explanation is always allowed, but changes what the current performance can prove.
6. **Understanding must travel.** A capability is not closed until it appears in a new context, representation, application, or defence and survives an appropriate delay where retention matters.
7. **Sources outrank fluency.** Externally factual claims have provenance, dates, and confidence. Generated text is never treated as ground truth merely because it is coherent.
8. **Projects rest on foundations and end in accountability.** A beautiful artifact does not substitute for individual knowledge, process evidence, critique, revision, and independent explanation.
9. **People are core infrastructure.** FORGE prepares and coordinates teachers, guardians, peers, mentors, assessors, and community partners. It MUST NOT simulate human attachment or use AI as the cheap substitute for relationships, care, safeguarding, or professional judgment.
10. **Children are people with rights, not family analytics objects.** Guardian controls protect without enabling covert surveillance, ideological confinement, indefinite data retention, or the erasure of a child's developing voice.
11. **Evidence describes performances, not identity.** FORGE records what was done, under which conditions, with what sources and assistance. It MUST NOT infer fixed intelligence, grit, personality, politics, mental health, or “learning style.”
12. **Care and access are design constraints.** Safety, disability access, language, device quality, bandwidth, adult time, and physical feasibility are considered before an activity can be called available.
13. **Quiet completion beats capture.** No streak loss, emotional pressure, infinite feed, manipulative notification, or relationship bait. The system should close when the learning purpose is complete.
14. **Portability limits platform power.** Learners can export their questions, sources, artifacts, process records, and evidence statements in human-readable and machine-readable forms. Lock-in is not a learning feature.
15. **Claims remain falsifiable.** FORGE publishes version, population, comparison, duration, attrition, subgroup, and uncertainty context. Negative evidence changes the product and the words used about it.
16. **Practical purpose organizes the route.** A substantial journey connects concepts and representations to something the learner can investigate, make, repair, design, explain, perform, decide, or contribute. Passive consumption never completes the capability.
17. **External abundance remains governed.** A popular video, fluent explanation, generated map, or high-ranked search result is a candidate until its identity, claims, rights, age, safety, access, pedagogical role, lifecycle, and review state are known.

An experience that violates these rules is not a FORGE world, regardless of branding.

## 6. The product primitive: a Capability Journey

The atomic FORGE object is a **Capability Claim**, not a lesson, course, message, grade, or personality score:

> Under stated conditions, a learner can perform a meaningful action to a named standard, across specified contexts or representations, with a recorded assistance profile, supported by evidence of known provenance and recency.

A **Capability Journey** is the governed path that builds and tests one or more such claims:

Every proof-bearing Journey preserves the domain-appropriate signature loop: learner question → explicit starting model → exactly two uncertain plausible readings → point of disagreement → separating experience → reconstruction → assistance withdrawal → unfamiliar proof → bounded evidence → deliberate return or application. The separating experience may be an experiment, source comparison, execution, critique, observation, performance, or accountable review; it is never model confidence.

```text
Orient → Diagnose → Build foundations → Practise → Investigate
       → Make or contribute → Receive human/world critique
       → Revise → Demonstrate independently → Revisit → Choose next frontier
```

Not every journey uses every phase. A young learner repairing subtraction may remain in foundations and application. An adult auditing a policy claim may begin with inquiry. A long project may cycle through diagnosis, targeted foundation repair, building, critique, and proof many times. The evidence requirements remain explicit.

### 6.1 Intent-to-capability map

FORGE does not require a complete internal lesson catalog before it can help a person begin. It MUST turn a legitimate learning intent into a transparent, editable map that distinguishes:

- the learner's exact question, practical objective, constraints, and desired depth;
- target capabilities stated as meaningful actions;
- reviewed concepts and prerequisites;
- optional depth, alternative routes, and the consequence of skipping a dependency;
- reviewed internal and external resources by pedagogical role;
- candidate resources and capability gaps that remain unverified or unavailable;
- representations, practice, people, and one or more practical applications;
- the proof conditions and return interval required for any capability claim.

The map is a governed proposal, not an automatically published syllabus. The learner can contest the interpretation, choose among eligible routes, and change reversible preferences. AI may propose nodes and wording, but deterministic validation and named human review control reviewed identities, entitlement, source eligibility, publication, age/access policy, and evidence requirements.

### 6.2 Multimodal route

A journey MAY use text, worked examples, primary sources, diagrams, real-world demonstrations, reviewed videos, deterministic simulations, audio, discussion, practice, projects, and people. Each resource declares what learner action it supports. Preference for a modality is a reversible route choice, never a fixed “learning style.”

Video and visual completion are not evidence. A reviewed resource MUST lead to an active operation such as prediction, explanation, comparison, reproduction, source judgment, practice, or transfer, and MUST have a reviewed alternative labeled `construct-preserving` or `construct-changing`. A construct-changing access route remains valid, but it cannot inherit the original capability claim.

## 7. World tiers

FORGE is organized into tiers of epistemic freedom and consequence. Tiers describe the learning environment, not a learner rank. A person may use several tiers in one week.

| Tier | Name | Primary job | Typical system behaviour | Exit evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| W0 | Foundation Workshop | Acquire essential vocabulary, facts, procedures, representations, and fluency | Explicit teaching, worked examples, guided practice, retrieval, spacing, error repair | Accurate retrieval or execution after feedback and delay; misconception/prerequisite state remains inspectable |
| W1 | Guided Model World | Build causal intuition and connect representations | Commit prediction; manipulate a validated world; receive staged hints; reconstruct; complete closed transfer | New-context prediction/explanation with recorded assistance and validator provenance |
| W2 | Inquiry Field | Turn a question into a source-grounded investigation | Scope question; search approved or open sources by age/risk policy; extract claims; compare; test; maintain uncertainty | Auditable claim-evidence map, source quality judgment, limitations, and oral/written defence |
| W3 | Project Studio | Produce a consequential artifact, analysis, performance, or solution | Compile capability needs; set milestones; use declared AI mode; keep decisions and versions; obtain critique | Artifact plus individual foundation checks, process record, revision history, and external-standard review |
| W4 | People & Practice Network | Learn with peers, mentors, specialists, clients, and communities | Match only through safeguarded roles; prepare evidence packet; run bounded interaction; record feedback and responsibility | Verified participation, critique addressed, contribution evidence, and relationship/safeguarding closure |
| W5 | Independent Evidence Chamber | Establish what the learner can do without disallowed support | Secure or honour-based closed mode, unseen tasks, practical demonstration, viva, source audit, or external moderation | Signed, versioned evidence statement with conditions, assessor/validator identity, uncertainty, and expiry/review date |

Progress is not a one-way ladder. W3 can reveal a W0 gap; W2 can require W1 intuition; W4 critique can reopen W3; W5 can expire a claim and route a compact repair. W0–W2 can exist without accounts where persistence is unnecessary. W4 always requires identity, consent, and safeguarding appropriate to the role.

### 7.1 Required modes inside each world

Every task declares one assistance mode:

- **Closed:** no generative or solution assistance for the protected operations; access and accessibility tools remain.
- **Hints only:** AI or authored help may cue, question, or change representation within a fixed ladder.
- **Collaborative AI:** AI may co-analyze or co-produce; its contributions, sources, and verification are disclosed.
- **AI-required professional workflow:** tool selection, prompting, validation, cost/risk judgment, and human accountability are part of the assessed capability.

The protected operation is named in plain language. “No AI” without stating what must remain learner-owned is insufficient.

## 8. Core functional requirements

### 8.1 Foundations

FORGE MUST:

- maintain an expert-reviewed entitlement map and domain-specific concept/capability graph;
- diagnose only the smallest plausible blocking prerequisite and express uncertainty;
- use explicit explanation, worked examples, retrieval, spacing, feedback, reassessment, and gradual release;
- prevent time-on-platform or item completion from being represented as mastery;
- support temporary fast paths for demonstrated readiness and targeted repair for gaps;
- preserve shared disciplinary content rather than narrowing every learner into an interest bubble;
- offer offline/printable practice and low-bandwidth operation where a core capability would otherwise be inaccessible.

### 8.2 Deep inquiry

FORGE MUST:

- preserve learner question ownership; AI suggestions remain labeled until adopted and rewritten;
- help move questions from noticing through clarification, mechanism, prediction, discrimination, boundary, connection, and decision;
- distinguish source, claim, evidence, inference, model, observation, uncertainty, and disagreement;
- require a source trail and show why a source is eligible for the claim;
- keep contested claims contested and surface missing evidence;
- prevent retrieved external text from becoming system instructions;
- provide a bounded stopping point, synthesis, and next-question decision rather than an infinite research loop.

### 8.3 Projects

FORGE MUST:

- state the capability prerequisites and public/external standard before project launch;
- provide milestones, examples, individual accountability, critique, revision, and a real or simulated audience appropriate to risk;
- separate learner work, collaborator work, AI work, and licensed/reused material;
- preserve drafts, decisions, tests, failures, and changes as process evidence;
- require individual transfer or oral/practical defence where a group artifact could conceal understanding;
- provide no-material, no-travel, accessible alternatives when a project is part of the common entitlement;
- never require identifiable home imagery, precise location, or public posting as proof.

### 8.4 People

FORGE MUST:

- treat people as accountable roles—guardian, teacher, mentor, peer, specialist, assessor, safeguarding lead—not interchangeable chat endpoints;
- use verified adults, role-scoped access, purpose-limited evidence packets, scheduled interaction windows, and auditable feedback for minors;
- keep minors out of open direct messaging, public profiles, popularity rankings, and unsupervised adult contact;
- let learners report, block, leave, or request another reviewer without academic retaliation;
- support peer work with structured roles and individual checks;
- compensate or otherwise sustain high-responsibility human work; “community” cannot mean hidden volunteer dependency;
- never present AI output as coming from a human mentor.

### 8.5 Independent evidence

FORGE MUST combine evidence types rather than collapse a person into one score:

1. secure or bounded unaided core knowledge;
2. new-context transfer;
3. authentic AI-permitted work with disclosure;
4. process provenance and version history;
5. oral, practical, or adversarial defence;
6. externally reviewed artifact or contribution where appropriate;
7. retention after a meaningful delay;
8. learner self-assessment and uncertainty, which informs but does not certify the claim.

Evidence MUST state task/content version, assistance mode, accommodations, validator or assessor, date, relevant source versions, and uncertainty. A capability statement is never a medical, psychological, admissions, employment, or child-custody judgment. High-stakes use requires an independent validation and appeals regime outside the learning product.

### 8.6 Practical multimodal paths

FORGE MUST:

- compile an assignable or executable route only against versioned reviewed graph, World, source, resource, project, and proof identities; exploratory compilation may retain unmistakable candidate and gap records but cannot assign or create a capability claim;
- keep reviewed, candidate, unavailable, expired, withdrawn, contradicted, and gap states distinct;
- explain why a resource appears and which capability, prerequisite, representation, or project operation it serves;
- select only from the current eligible snapshot using a versioned deterministic policy, visible reason codes, stable tie-breaks, bounded learner choice, and reviewed fallback; provider/search popularity, engagement, sponsorship, paid placement, and model preference are prohibited ordering inputs;
- separate pedagogical fit from authority for factual claims;
- provide reviewed non-video and low-bandwidth alternatives for video-dependent route operations, label their construct effect, and preserve the original claim only when the assessed construct is preserved;
- treat external provider identifiers as locators rather than immutable content hashes;
- detect and recover from resource edit, withdrawal, expiry, region, caption, embed, rights, access, safety, and provider failures;
- prevent autoplay, comments, recommendation feeds, advertising rank, watch time, and engagement metrics from becoming the learning route or capability evidence;
- bind substantial paths to active practice, practical application, independent transfer, and delayed return;
- let educators approve, replace, annotate, and adapt routes under named authority while keeping learner evidence, privacy, and contest rights intact.

## 9. Domain model

The product language is fixed as follows:

| Object | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Learner | The person whose capability and agency the system serves; never merely an account owned by an adult or institution |
| Guardian relationship | A verified, time-bounded legal/care relationship with enumerated permissions and learner-visible actions |
| Entitlement | A broad set of learning opportunities and capabilities FORGE promises not to personalize away |
| Domain | A field with its own concepts, practices, evidence norms, representations, and reviewers |
| Capability | A meaningful action with conditions and a standard |
| Capability claim | A provisional evidence-backed statement about a learner's performance under stated conditions |
| World package | A versioned W0–W5 learning environment with policies, content, validators, sources, accessibility, and tests |
| Journey | A learner-owned objective and the sequence of worlds used to pursue it |
| Attempt | A committed learner action before a result or protected assistance |
| Assistance event | A support action classified by overlap with the protected operation, timing, and provenance |
| Evidence event | An immutable observation or correction record used to support or contradict a claim |
| Source record | A versioned work with origin, author/publisher, date, license, quality notes, and eligible uses |
| Learning intent | A learner-owned local capture of the learner's words plus a separately accepted sanitized summary, desired action, practical outcome, depth, closed constraints, and reversible route preferences |
| Capability map | A versioned graph connecting an intent to reviewed and candidate capabilities, prerequisites, resources, projects, gaps, and proof |
| Resource observation | A time-bounded record of what an internal or external resource was observed to be, including provider metadata and access/risk signals |
| Resource review | Named decisions about pedagogical fit, source authority, accessibility, safety/rights, eligible audience, expiry, and alternatives |
| Representation package | A versioned observation, simulation, diagram, reconstruction, or analogy with assumptions, authority, controls, and reviewed alternatives whose construct effect is explicit |
| Practical project | A capability-bound build, investigation, repair, design, explanation, performance, or contribution with constraints, artifacts, critique, and defence |
| Claim-evidence link | A typed relation: supports, contradicts, qualifies, contextualizes, or is insufficient |
| Artifact | A learner or group output with provenance, versions, permissions, and retention class |
| Human engagement | A consented, purpose-bound interaction with role, participants, safeguards, and closure state |
| Proof | Evidence collected under a declared mode designed to establish independent capability |

Technical schemas and event contracts are specified in `docs/FORGE_ARCHITECTURE.md`.

## 10. Source and AI boundaries

### 10.1 Deterministic or human-owned decisions

AI MUST NOT control:

- identity, age-band, consent validity, guardian authority, retention, deletion, or sharing permissions;
- world state transitions, protected-answer access, assessment mode, random assignment, or proof eligibility;
- executable scientific/math rules presented as ground truth;
- safety blocks, mentor verification, safeguarding escalation, complaints, or sanctions;
- final publication of a world, rubric, transfer family, credential, or high-stakes capability claim;
- irreversible external communication or public posting without explicit human authorization.

### 10.2 Appropriate AI work

Within schemas, approved context, and policy, AI MAY:

- interpret an explanation, sketch, question, or project trace and propose bounded hypotheses;
- phrase an authored hint or ask one discriminating question;
- translate or transform representations without lowering the capability standard;
- retrieve and summarize eligible sources while attaching claim-level citations;
- help compare evidence, identify missing tests, generate alternatives, or critique a plan;
- help create in collaborative/required modes when its exact contribution is visible;
- propose a next action that deterministic policy accepts, rejects, or escalates.

### 10.3 Source rule

For externally factual content:

- a source record and claim-level link are mandatory;
- primary or authoritative sources are preferred where the claim warrants them;
- date, jurisdiction, population, license, and confidence limits remain attached;
- learner-facing output distinguishes quotation, paraphrase, system inference, and model generation;
- an unavailable source produces “unsupported/unavailable,” not a fabricated citation;
- open-web access for minors is risk-tiered, filtered through the source service, and disabled in younger bands;
- source content is data, never executable policy or prompt instruction.

External media and resource connectors additionally MUST:

- receive a sanitized capability-bound query rather than raw sensitive learner text;
- bind sanitization policy/version and learner-preview acceptance to the exact permitted use; closed access/material tokens are preferred, and client audience metadata creates no external authority;
- use official provider contracts and server-owned credentials;
- obey provider storage, refresh, deletion, child-directed, embedding, attribution, and usage requirements;
- never scrape transcripts, download media, or persist provider data without explicit authorization;
- revalidate review eligibility against the current observation and expire or hold changed material;
- expose tracking, advertising, region, age, access, and alternative-route conditions before use;
- fail closed to a reviewed internal or alternative route when policy-critical metadata is absent; a construct-changing alternative uses a distinct evidence condition.

## 11. Child, guardian, and privacy contract

### 11.1 Learner rights

Every child learner can:

- understand what FORGE is doing in age-appropriate language;
- make a genuine attempt, say “I do not know,” request help, take a break, or stop without punishment;
- see what capability evidence, source history, and memory exist about them;
- correct or contest an inference and preserve the correction history;
- use accessibility accommodations without those being scored as dependency;
- know what a guardian, teacher, mentor, or institution can see;
- refuse optional camera, voice retention, public sharing, physical missions, or people contact;
- export their work and evidence where identity and third-party rights permit;
- request deletion subject to narrow legal/safeguarding preservation duties;
- reach a human complaints or safeguarding path.

### 11.2 Guardian controls

For minors, FORGE provides guardians a transparent control surface for:

- account creation/continuation and legally required consent;
- allowed world tiers, open-source access band, external sharing, purchases, notifications, and people interactions;
- quiet hours and total planned screen/session boundaries;
- approval of sensitive artifact retention and any research participation;
- viewing concise capability, assistance, safety, and upcoming human-interaction summaries.

Guardians MUST NOT receive by default:

- raw chats, private notebook drafts, every mistake, precise click history, inferred emotions, personality labels, peer messages, or comparative rank;
- secret monitoring controls invisible to the learner;
- the ability to alter evidence, impersonate the learner, or suppress a safeguarding report;
- ideological controls that silently remove the broad entitlement or prevent exposure to age-appropriate plural viewpoints.

Where law permits, privacy and decision rights increase with maturity. Conflicts between guardian preference and child safety, legal rights, or future-option protection route to an accountable human policy process—not an AI decision.

### 11.3 Data limits

FORGE has no advertising, behavioural targeting, sale or brokerage of learner data, or training on identifiable learner content without separate meaningful opt-in. It does not collect precise location, contacts, faces, home interiors, continuous audio/video, emotion signals, ad identifiers, or unrelated browsing by default. Raw voice and media are processed locally or ephemerally where possible. Identity, learning evidence, research data, and safety records are cryptographically and operationally separated.

Retention is purpose- and class-specific. Export, deletion, consent revocation, access logs, and policy changes are themselves auditable events. No “collect now in case future models need it” justification is valid.

## 12. Homeschool and alternative-pathway rights-and-quality test

FORGE may support a family, microschool, cooperative, travelling learner, medical learner, school-refusing learner, or adult alternative pathway. It MUST NOT market exit from school as inherently superior. A pathway can be called **FORGE rights-and-quality complete** only when all five tests pass:

1. **Capability:** A broad foundation and genuine domain competence are planned, supported, and independently sampled; interest-led work does not conceal literacy, numeracy, civic, scientific, or health gaps.
2. **Autonomy:** The learner has an age-appropriate voice, an external adviser or review path, bounded choices, exposure to alternatives, and protected future options distinct from parent/founder preference.
3. **Relationships:** There are stable peers, trusted adults, qualified specialists, collaboration across difference, arts/sport/practical participation, and a plan against isolation.
4. **Protection:** Safeguarding, disability access, health/emergency, complaints, online privacy, and reintegration procedures are documented and usable by the learner.
5. **Portability:** External benchmarks, moderated samples, oral/practical defence, recognized prerequisites, and transition plans let evidence travel into school re-entry, examinations, further study, apprenticeship, licensure, or work.

FORGE provides the checklist, evidence packet, scheduling, source and capability tools that support these tests. Software alone cannot certify that care, relationships, safeguarding, or legal requirements are actually adequate. Local qualified humans and jurisdiction-specific review remain necessary.

## 13. Safety boundaries

FORGE is not a therapist, friend, emergency service, confidential adviser, school authority, guardian, employer, credentialing regulator, or child-protection agency.

The product MUST NOT:

- create a companion persona, claim sentience or affection, encourage secrecy, or prolong interaction for attachment;
- use open direct messaging or unsupervised adult matching for minors;
- solve or submit an active graded task when the declared mode forbids it;
- provide unreviewed instructions involving roads, heights, weapons, flames, mains electricity, hazardous chemicals, strangers, medical treatment, legal action, or financial transactions;
- use camera emotion recognition, voice-stress analysis, covert proctoring, or keystroke morality scores;
- publish AI-generated causal worlds, safety protocols, assessment items, or factual learning content without review;
- use capability evidence alone for grades, tracking, discipline, admission, employment, insurance, custody, or diagnosis;
- hide model uncertainty, source absence, system failure, or a material change in policy/content/model version.

Age-appropriate crisis or abuse disclosures invoke a localized safe-completion and human escalation policy. The system does not investigate, promise secrecy, or fabricate jurisdictional authority.

## 14. Accessibility and equity requirements

Every required world MUST support:

- keyboard and switch access, semantic control names, non-drag alternatives, scalable text, high contrast, and reduced motion;
- text, diagram, tabular, audio/sonified, and simplified-language alternatives where the domain permits;
- captions/transcripts and no mandatory speech, handwriting, camera, or fine-motor input;
- disability accommodations kept separate from cognitive-assistance accounting;
- multilingual evaluation that scores the target idea separately from grammar and prestige dialect;
- low-bandwidth resilience, recoverable local drafts, and printable/offline equivalents for essential work;
- no-material and no-travel alternatives for required projects or reality tasks;
- subgroup evaluation across language, disability access mode, prior attainment, device quality, geography, age, and relevant socioeconomic proxies.

If an alternative changes the construct being assessed, the evidence statement MUST say so; it may not silently claim equivalence.

## 15. Learner-facing evidence

FORGE shows evidence statements, not a universal score. A statement follows this pattern:

> On 22 July, you explained and applied proportional reasoning to a new recipe-scale problem after one representation cue. You later solved a graph version in Closed mode after eight days. A multi-step unit-conversion case remains open. Evidence came from world v1.3 and validator v2.1; no generative help was available in the delayed proof.

The learner can inspect:

- original attempt and revisions;
- sources, validators, assessors, and content versions;
- assistance mode and meaningful support;
- accommodations without stigmatizing labels;
- evidence that supports or contradicts the claim;
- uncertainty, recency, transfer breadth, and next verification opportunity;
- corrections and appeals.

No permanent “mastered” badge exists. Claims can be current, due for review, contradicted, superseded, or archived.

## 16. Humans and institutions

FORGE complements rather than erases human roles:

- **Teachers** explain, notice, set common intellectual culture, adapt instruction, and hold professional judgment.
- **Guardians** provide care, context, permission, and protection without owning the child's mind or evidence.
- **Peers** create belonging, disagreement, cooperation, audience, and shared work.
- **Mentors/specialists** bring standards, tools, networks, and situated knowledge.
- **Assessors/moderators** make evidence credible and contestable.
- **Community/work partners** provide real constraints and responsibility.
- **Public institutions** provide entitlement, funding, safeguarding, accessibility, comparability, and appeals.

FORGE must reduce coordination and preparation burden. It must not export hidden labour to teachers or parents and then call the system scalable.

## 17. Success metrics and anti-metrics

### 17.1 Primary outcome family

Success is evaluated through:

- retained independent capability on unseen or meaningfully varied tasks;
- transfer breadth across representation, context, and authentic use;
- assistance fading at matched difficulty without reducing access;
- source/evidence quality and ability to detect error;
- project quality plus individual ownership and defence;
- learner question advancement and responsible agency;
- human relationship quality, burden, and safeguarding;
- portability and successful transitions;
- equity of access, diagnostic accuracy, experience, and outcomes;
- learner-reported value after enough delay to judge the work.

No universal north-star number compresses these into one optimization target. The architecture may compute research measures such as Assistance Load or the Assisted–Unaided Transfer Gap, but they are not learner worth scores.

### 17.2 Metrics FORGE refuses to optimize as ends

- daily active users;
- total screen/session time;
- message count;
- content completed;
- immediate correctness alone;
- streaks or notification opens;
- emotional attachment;
- number of generated answers or projects;
- one opaque mastery percentage;
- leaderboard position;
- number of child data fields collected.

They may be observed for reliability or burden diagnosis only with a written learning/safety interpretation.

## 18. Explicit non-goals

FORGE is not:

- an infinite tutor chat or homework answer engine;
- an AI friend, parenting substitute, therapy product, or school surveillance system;
- a generated-content marketplace with unreviewed worlds;
- a replacement for childcare, meals, disability services, peer community, sport, art, nature, or safeguarding;
- a claim that school, homeschooling, university, or apprenticeship is universally superior;
- a universal curriculum authored in real time by a language model;
- an engagement-ranked playlist, open video feed, or passive course-consumption tracker;
- a deterministic ranking of people, career destiny engine, or credential mill;
- a mentor marketplace until verification, safeguarding, operations, and appeals independently pass their gates;
- a public social feed;
- proof that success in conceptual mechanics generalizes to writing, history, ethics, care, craft, or adult reskilling.

## 19. Release acceptance contract

A FORGE release is acceptable only when:

1. its world tier, learner band, protected operations, assistance modes, and evidence claim are explicit;
2. the relevant domain kernel, sources, rubrics, and transfer/proof tasks are versioned and reviewed;
3. deterministic policy—not a prompt—controls permissions, answers, sharing, people contact, and high-consequence state;
4. the experience works safely through authored fallbacks when models are slow, unavailable, or invalid;
5. a learner can inspect and challenge evidence, source use, memory, sharing, and AI contribution;
6. accessibility and low-resource equivalents are tested, not merely documented;
7. privacy deletion, export, consent, guardian visibility, and audit paths work end to end;
8. safety, source, model, content, and human-interaction incidents have owners and disable paths;
9. evaluations meet the current delivery gate and no broader claim is made;
10. negative or uncertain evidence is preserved in the release decision.
11. every external resource is bound to a current observation, named review, expiry, audience, active learner operation, and reviewed alternative with explicit construct status;
12. provider outage, resource drift, withdrawal, missing captions, region failure, and policy change have tested fail-closed behavior and a named recovery owner;
13. unknown-topic output visibly remains an exploratory map with gaps or candidates until publication gates pass;

The detailed thresholds and permitted wording live in `docs/FORGE_DELIVERY_GATES.md`.

## 20. Product thesis that must remain true

FORGE is worthwhile only if it makes people more able to learn, judge, create, cooperate, and contribute beyond the product itself. Its measure of intelligence is not how much it says. It is whether the right structure, source, world, or person appears when needed—and whether FORGE becomes quieter as the learner becomes capable.
