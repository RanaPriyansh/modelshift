**Status:** Governing rationale; source-grounded design synthesis, not an efficacy result

## 0. Purpose and evidence base

This document explains why FORGE has the product and architecture specified in `../FORGE_PRODUCT_SPEC.md` and `FORGE_ARCHITECTURE.md`. It is grounded in two local reports, both with a research cut-off of 19 July 2026:

1. `../rebuilding_education_for_the_ai_age.html` — broad diagnosis of education's purposes, failures, learning science, opt-out pathways, AI-era capabilities, reform portfolios, and institutional limits.
2. `../forge_ai_native_learning_system_report.html` — product selection, failure archaeology, Capability Loop, assistance governor, measurement, architecture, safety/privacy, pilots, and falsification plan.

The reports were read as structured syntheses, including their limitations and red teams. This document does not convert every cited study into a new causal claim. The reports' component evidence constrains the design; it does **not** validate the integrated FORGE system.

The 23 July 2026 practical-path amendment also uses current primary or official material recorded in [`program/AI_ERA_LEARNING_THESIS.md`](./program/AI_ERA_LEARNING_THESIS.md) and the repository research ledger. That supplemental research covers AI/work scenarios, active and multimodal learning, external-resource policy, platform lifecycle, teacher augmentation, and operating controls. It extends this traceability; it does not retroactively validate FORGE.

### Legacy traceability limitation

## 1. How to read the traceability

Each system decision is classified as one of:

- **Preserve:** a valuable educational function that software must protect or coordinate.
- **Countermeasure:** a product mechanism aimed at a documented failure.
- **Constraint:** a boundary imposed by weak evidence, child rights, safety, privacy, equity, or institutional reality.
- **Experiment:** a plausible mechanism that cannot be claimed effective until the delivery gates are passed.

Confidence refers to the strength of the research conclusion in the local reports, not confidence that FORGE will implement it successfully.

## 2. Governing synthesis

The two reports reject two symmetrical errors:

1. **Preserve the current progression logic because schools serve necessary functions.** This mistakes institutional value for adequacy of every mechanism.
2. **Replace schools, teachers, foundations, and common standards with personalized AI exploration.** This discards care, relationships, common knowledge, safeguarding, public accountability, and the efficient support novices need.

FORGE therefore means **replacement architecture for what education lacks**, not replacement childhood or replacement society. It replaces weak connective logic—seat time without mastery, answers without ownership, projects without foundations, evidence without provenance, personalization without entitlement, and credentials without inspectable capability. It preserves and coordinates human/public functions that a product cannot legitimately absorb.

## 5. Why the six world tiers follow from the research

### W0 — Foundation Workshop

The broad report places foundational literacy/numeracy and cumulative domain knowledge first. The learning-science sequence supports explanation, modelling, guided practice, retrieval, spacing, feedback, and reassessment. W0 exists so “agency” does not mean leaving a novice to search blindly.

### W1 — Guided Model World

The product report identifies a tractable missing primitive: a committed model meets a deterministic or otherwise validated consequence, assistance is staged, and transfer occurs after help closes. W1 generalizes that structure beyond the historical mechanics wedge only when each domain supplies a valid kernel and transfer theory.

### W2 — Inquiry Field

The reports argue that question quality, source provenance, epistemic discipline, and boundary awareness become more valuable in the AI age. W2 opens inquiry after enough knowledge exists and makes claims, sources, uncertainty, and stopping rules visible.

### W3 — Project Studio

Project evidence is positive but heterogeneous and vulnerable to shallow artifacts, group free-riding, parental substitution, and AI production. W3 makes prerequisites, milestones, contribution provenance, critique, revision, and individual accountability structural.

### W4 — People & Practice Network

Exceptional learning lives and system evidence repeatedly point to mentors, peers, tools, institutions, and real responsibility. The same evidence warns that access is unequal and child contact is risky. W4 is a separately gated human-operations system, not a social feature.

### W5 — Independent Evidence Chamber

AI makes unaudited outputs weak evidence. Both reports converge on secure core checks, process, authentic tool use, oral/practical defence, external moderation, and delayed transfer. W5 states conditions precisely rather than pretending one format proves the whole person.

Passing the test is a human/institutional judgment supported by evidence. FORGE MUST use “evidence complete for review,” not “certified safe/complete,” unless a lawful independent authority supplies that certification.

## 9. Domain expansion rules

The mechanics report is explicit that physics may be unusually favourable. Expansion requires a new **domain representation and evidence theory**, not a new topic prompt.

Before a domain enters W1–W5, reviewers must specify:

1. What counts as knowledge, practice, judgment, and independent capability in this domain?
2. Which consequences are executable, observable, source-grounded, convention-governed, or legitimately contestable?
3. What representations must a capable person coordinate?
4. Which novice errors or alternative models are common enough to author responsibly?
5. What assistance would perform the protected operation?
6. What constitutes near transfer, broader transfer, authentic application, and delayed retention?
7. Which outcomes require a domain professional or community standard rather than software scoring?
8. What safety, cultural, political, or ethical disagreements must remain visible?
9. How will linguistic, disability, and cultural variation affect evaluation?
10. What strong active baseline will FORGE be compared against?

Examples:

- mathematics/physics may use deterministic kernels and symbolic validators;
- computing may use sandboxed execution, tests, traces, and code review;
- history uses source criticism, chronology, competing interpretations, and oral/written argument—not a generated “historical simulator” as truth;
- writing uses audience, rhetorical purpose, drafts, editorial judgment, and defence—not grammar/style alone;
- design/art uses constraints, craft, critique, iteration, and contextual judgment—not a single correctness score;
- care, trades, clinical, or physical practice requires supervised real-world performance and cannot be certified from screen traces.

## 10. Evaluation implications derived from the research

The integrated FORGE hypothesis remains experimental. Evaluations must therefore:

- compare with a strong authored/teacher-guided baseline, not only no instruction;
- compare governed AI with realistic ordinary AI use;
- measure delayed unseen unaided performance, not only supported completion;
- validate the assessment before using it to validate the product;
- pre-register primary outcomes, task families, exclusions, subgroup analyses, and stop rules;
- report intention-to-treat, attrition, time, workload, cost, model/content/source versions, and incidents;
- treat fixed-script equivalence as a reason to remove model complexity;
- test whether projects, curiosity, or people layers add value without sacrificing foundations or widening access gaps;
- publish null/negative results and narrow claims after failed redesign;
- require independent replication before institutional or cross-domain claims.

The detailed claim ladder and gates are in `FORGE_DELIVERY_GATES.md`.

## 11. Research limitations preserved as architecture constraints

| Limitation in the reports | Architecture consequence |
|---|---|
| Component evidence does not validate the complete FORGE package | modular events and experiments isolate mechanisms; no package-efficacy claim before comparative trials |
| AI evidence is young, model-specific, and perishable | model/version ledger, provider abstraction, held-out evals, canaries, authored fallback |
| Many studies use immediate or local outcomes | delayed proof, external task families, retention, and independent replication |
| Meta-analytic effects combine heterogeneous contexts | domain/population/version-specific claims; no effect-size copy-paste into product promises |
| Homeschool and alternative-school outcomes have major selection bias | rights-and-quality review and no superiority claim |
| Biographies identify conditions, not average effects | tools, notebooks, mentors, projects become opportunities—not “genius formulas” |
| International comparisons are observational and context-bound | configurable entitlements/local review; no “copy country X” operating model |
| Physical missions and people access are unequal | no-material/access equivalents, transport/equipment burden metrics, separately gated W4 |
| Company-owned instruction and assessment can overfit | item contamination controls, external assessment, preregistration, independent evaluators |
| Privacy/safeguarding law varies by jurisdiction | policy versions, regional review, fail-closed child features; no universal compliance claim |
| Authoring economics are unknown | package-level author/review time, defect rates, and stop thresholds |

## 12. Research questions that remain open

The following are not design details to be assumed away:

1. Does a governed Capability Journey outperform excellent authored instruction at comparable active time on delayed transfer?
2. Which learner bands and domains benefit from model-mediated diagnosis beyond fixed authored routes?
3. Will learners tolerate protected cognitive work without moving to a second answer tool, and under which motivations/distribution contexts?
4. Can world packages be authored, reviewed, translated, made accessible, and maintained at sustainable quality and cost?
5. How reliable can open explanation, inquiry, project, and oral/practical evidence become without unfairly rewarding language, resources, or adult help?
6. What guardian-control design protects children while respecting privacy, voice, plural knowledge, and future options?
7. Can homeschool and microschool review improve quality without becoming surveillance or pretending weak telemetry establishes safeguarding?
8. Can a people network deliver qualified, diverse, reliable human contact without unacceptable safety risk, burden, or socioeconomic capture?
9. Do learner-owned question and evidence graphs increase long-term agency, or do they become an instrumented shadow record?
10. Which evidence packets are actually trusted and portable across teachers, schools, universities, employers, and regulators?
11. How much closed-tool capability is necessary in each domain and profession when tools are ubiquitous but fallible?
12. Does FORGE reduce inequality, or does it mostly amplify families already rich in time, devices, knowledge, and mentors?

Each requires a named gate, comparison, outcome, subgroup view, ethical review, and stopping rule.

## 13. Exact research-backed claim boundary

Based on the two reports, FORGE may currently say:

> FORGE is designed around converging evidence that foundations, guided practice, retrieval, feedback, varied application, human relationships, source judgment, and increasing independence matter, and around direct warnings that AI-supported performance can diverge from later unaided learning.

FORGE may not currently say:

- that the integrated FORGE architecture improves learning;
- that it is better than school, homeschooling, tutoring, a strong authored course, or ordinary AI;
- that its capability claims are psychometrically valid, portable credentials;
- that guardian controls, source retrieval, people systems, or privacy architecture are safe/compliant in any jurisdiction;
- that success in ModelShift or mechanics generalizes to another domain, age band, or institution;
- that a learner's evidence graph predicts intelligence, future success, character, or career fit;
- that building the specified system is operationally or economically sustainable.

Those claims become eligible only through the corresponding delivery gates. Research grounding is a reason to test the architecture carefully, not permission to market the result in advance.

## 14. Practical-path research amendment

The founder's new practical-path direction creates five additional system questions:

1. Can FORGE help with an unfamiliar topic without pretending to own or validate a complete course?
2. When does video or another visual representation improve understanding rather than create passive familiarity?
3. How can an external resource remain useful when its content, rights, tracking, captions, region, or availability changes?
4. How should AI assistance complement teachers and independent learning without replacing protected learner work?
5. Which AI-era product decisions remain good under slow, fast, concentrated, or broadly shared capability progress?

### 14.2 Operating-method translation

The practical-path supply chain also adapts mature operating methods:

- immutable package revisions and independent approval for high-risk changes;
- author, specialist, whole-product, accessibility, and safety/rights review as separate responsibilities;
- candidate, reviewed, anchored, and published-reviewed/assignable maturity tiers;
- correction, withdrawal, incident hold, reporting, appeal, and rollback;
- error budgets that freeze catalog expansion when availability, correction, safety, access, or review reliability falls below the accepted threshold;
- total cost and workload accounting, including review, licensing, accessibility, professional learning, provider failure, and incident recovery.

These are operating controls, not learning evidence.

For operating examples only, [OpenStax's faculty-contributor and peer-review process](https://help.openstax.org/s/article/Do-OpenStax-textbooks-favor-any-particular-schools-of-thought-or-specific-teaching-approaches), [errata correction process](https://help.openstax.org/s/article/Errata-correction-process), and [accessibility guidance](https://help.openstax.org/s/article/Do-OpenStax-materials-meet-ADA-guidelines), along with [Open edX's explicit exercise/tool enablement](https://docs.openedx.org/en/latest/educators/concepts/exercise_tools/about_problems_exercises_tools.html), inform reviewed revisions, correction paths, accessibility status, and explicit activation boundaries. They are maintainer practices and tooling examples—not learning-effect evidence, product endorsement, or exhaustive traceability for FORGE.
